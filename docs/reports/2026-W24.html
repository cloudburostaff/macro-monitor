Executive Summary§ 1
The U.S. economy enters mid-June in an energy-driven, mildly stagflationary posture — yet, for now, a relative-price shock rather than a self-reinforcing spiral. An oil spike (Iran / Strait of Hormuz) has lifted headline inflation to multi-year highs — CPI +4.2% y/y, PPI +6.5% y/y — while underlying core measures and wage growth keep easing, signalling demand is contained. The labour market is still firm on current data (payrolls +172K) even as its forward indicators soften. Abroad, the ECB delivered its first rate rise since 2023, confirming a hawkish policy turn. Attention now shifts to next week's Federal Reserve meeting — the debut of new Chair Kevin Warsh — the pivotal event for the rate path.

What the Data Is Saying§ 3

Inflation — hot headline, cooling core

The energy shock is inflating top-line prices across the board: CPI +4.2% y/y (energy +23.5% y/y) and PPI +6.5% y/y, the hottest since late 2022. Beneath the surface, the Fed-relevant gauges are easing — core CPI +0.2% m/m and core PPI +0.4% m/m, both below expectations and the softest in months. The read: a supply-led, commodity move — not broad demand pressure. Next test: May PCE on 25 June.

Energy lifts the headline figures, but core (ex food & energy) sits well below — the signature of a price shock, not a spiral.

Labour & growth — firm today, softening at the edges

Hiring is solid on current data: May payrolls beat at +172K, unemployment steady at 4.3%, and wage growth contained (AHE +3.4% y/y, below inflation) — the equity-friendlier "strong jobs without hot wages" mix. But forward indicators are cooling together: jobless claims have drifted to 229K (highest since February), the JOLTS hiring rate has slipped, and ISM employment is below 50 in both manufacturing and services. An early-warning alignment — not yet a confirmed turn. Growth, meanwhile, is re-firming after a soft Q1 (GDP +1.6%): ISM new orders are strong and factory output rebounded, though business capex has lost momentum.

Housing — mixed and rate-sensitive

Lagging demand is firming (existing-home sales +3.2%, best pace since December, on improved affordability), but leading gauges remain soft and the 30-yr mortgage has backed up to 6.44%. May permits and starts (16 Jun) are the next tell.

Policy — a hawkish pivot abroad, the Fed in focus

The ECB raised rates +25bp on 11 June to a 2.25% deposit rate — its first hike since 2023 — lifting inflation forecasts even as it trimmed growth, a textbook stagflationary move. The Fed held at 3.50–3.75% in April, but its latest Beige Book shows energy costs broadening. Next week's FOMC (16–17 Jun) is Chair Warsh's debut and carries a fresh "dot plot"; a hold is near-certain, so the market's focus is his tone and the projected path. The BoE follows on 18 June.
