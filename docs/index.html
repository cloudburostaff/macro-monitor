Macro Monitor

A weekly, plain-English read on where the economy and policy are heading.

Macro Monitor is an automated macroeconomic surveillance service. A swarm of seven specialist agents continuously tracks the data that moves markets — inflation (CPI, PPI, PCE), the labour market (payrolls, jobless claims, JOLTS), growth and activity (GDP, ISM surveys, retail sales), housing, business and consumer sentiment, central-bank policy (Fed, ECB, BoE), and financial conditions. Each agent reads official releases at the primary source the moment they land and records a concise, verified digest.

A central Macro Strategist then synthesises every domain into one coherent read of the economic cycle — connecting the leads and lags between indicators, separating genuine signal from noise, and judging where the cycle and policy are heading. The result is a clear regime call, an explicit watch-list of what would confirm or deny a turn, and the week-ahead calendar.

Every Saturday that synthesis is published here as a five-minute executive briefing: plain business language, the numbers that matter, and the cross-asset implications. Past editions are archived below, so the evolving macro narrative can be followed week to week.
